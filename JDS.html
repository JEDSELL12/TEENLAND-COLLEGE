Subjects and their topics

  1. Carbohydrate
    • Meaning:Carbohydrates are organic compounds made up of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. They are one of the primary macronutrients, along with proteins and fats and serve as a key source energy for the body.


    • Glucose (a sugar that is the body's main energy source) Sucrose (table sugar Fructose) (found in fruits) Lactose (found in milk)




  2. Protein
    • Meaning: Proteins are essential for the growth and repair of tissues, and they play a crucial role in enzymes, hormones, and immune function.
    • Meat
    • Fish
    • Eggs
    • Milk
    • Beans
    • Lentils
    • Nuts
    • Seeds
    • Cheese
  3. Fats
    • Meaning:Fats are a concentrated source of energy, help in the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A,D,E,andK), and are important for cell structure and function.
    • meat fats
    • cheese
    • fatty fish (like salmon)
    • avocado
    • seeds
    • nuts
    • oil
    • Butter
  4. Vitamin
    • Meaning:Vitamins are organic compounds that are necessary for various metabolic processes, including immune support, energy production,and maintaining skin, bone, and eye health.
    • Vitamin A: Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach
    • Vitamin C: Oranges, strawberries, bell peppers
    • Vitamin D: Sunlight, fortified milk, fatty fish
    • Vitamin K: Leafy greens, broccoli, kale
    • Vitamin B complex: Whole grains, eggs, meat, legumes, bananas
    • Minerals
      • Meaning:Minerals are inorganic elements that help in processes such as building bones and teeth, muscle function,and nerve transmission.
      • Calcium: Dairy products, leafy greens, tofu
      • Iron: Red meat, spinach, legumes, fortified cereals
      • Potassium: Bananas, potatoes, spinach, beans
      • Magnesium: Nuts, seeds, whole grains, leafy greens
    • Water
      • Meaning: Water is crucial for hydration, digestion, temperature regulation, and the transport of nutrients and waste products throughout the body.
      • Water
      • soups
      • fruits (like watermelon)
      • vegetables (like cucumbers)
    • Dietary Fiber
      • Meaning:Fiber helps in digestion, regulates blood sugar levels, and promotes gut health. It is important for the prevention of constipation and certain diseases.
      • Whole grains
      • fruits
      • vegetables
      • legumes
      • nuts.
