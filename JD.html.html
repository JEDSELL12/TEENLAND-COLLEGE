Delicious Jollof Rice Recipe

Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, and a variety of spices. Here's a simple recipe to make it!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 3 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 red bell pepper (blended)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger powder
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine the tomatoes, red bell pepper, and half of the chopped onion. Blend until smooth.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the remaining chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  4. Add the blended tomato mixture to the pot and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate from the tomato paste.
  5. Stir in the thyme, curry powder, garlic powder, ginger powder, bay leaf, white pepper, and tomato paste. Cook for 2 minutes, then add the broth, salt, and sugar (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. Once the sauce is boiling, add the rinsed rice and stir to combine. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the rice cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. If necessary, add a little more water or broth during cooking.
  7. Optional: In the last 5 minutes of cooking, stir in the frozen peas and let them cook with the rice.
  8. Once the rice is done, remove the pot from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork.
